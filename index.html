RESEARCH

We began our case study by conducting one-on-one interviews with veterans. This allowed us to understand and empathize with the struggle veterans face to find a new career path.

We then created a survey to distribute to veterans. The data we collected uncovered that most veterans are unaware of all the benefits and resources available to them.

Our next phase was to do a competitor analysis. For the most part, the competitors covered essential points in helping veterans. We found an opportunity to make the NVO stand out by turning its website into one more desirable and easy to use than the others.

DEFINE

This allowed us to illustrate the user's pain points and the solution.

we used a user flow diagram to visualize our user's experience. We created a simple path for employment by matching his skills with new career opportunities.

THE PROBLEM:

Disabled veterans struggle to obtain resources for finding a new career path, receiving housing assistance, and coexisting in civilian life.

THE SOLUTION:

Redesign the National Veterans Outreach to make a website that encourages veterans to find the services they need.

DESIGN

Based on our user flow we sketched our prototype and received feedback for iteration.

We then incorporated the feedback and created a low-fidelity prototype to conduct user testing.

User testing allowed us to understand how we can improve our user’s experience.

After collecting the data we created a mid-fidelity prototype that adapted to our user’s priorities and increased affordances.

ITERATE

After conducting 10 user tests for our mid and High fidelity prototypes we received feedback for further iteration.

We implemented a mobile-first approach by incorporating fluid grids, considering the breakpoints for desktop and mobile.

We also implemented minimalist elements, giving priority to the content we wanted to display. In this case Veteran's Employment, Health Benefits and Housing.
